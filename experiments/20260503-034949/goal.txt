GOAL: Increase training length perpetually without full collapse.

Each iteration should push the training window longer (in 5-minute increments past the current stable point) while keeping the policy head from fully collapsing onto a single illegal move. "Full collapse" is defined by these signals in the results JSON:

(1) candidate_tests[].policy_head.policy_stats.illegal_mass_sum sustained ≥ 0.99 (essentially all probability mass on illegal moves) at the END of the run
(2) candidate_tests[].policy_head.policy_stats.max sustained ≥ 0.99 (one-hot collapse) at the END of the run
(3) candidate_tests[].policy_head.top1_legal_ever_positive == FALSE for the entire run (the network never put non-trivial mass on any legal move)

A run is acceptable as long as it does not exhibit full collapse by the end of its window. Partial illegal_mass_sum or partial max_prob during the run is fine — what matters is that the network has not flatlined.

There is **NO upper limit on training time**. The whole point is longer and longer runtimes. Aggressive jumps are encouraged: if the prior run was healthy, jumping straight to 60 minutes (3600 s), 90 minutes (5400 s), or longer is fine.

ACCEPT a proposal when:
- The run completed its full requested window without legal_mass_collapse early-bail, AND
- It did not show full collapse by the three definitions above, AND
- Either the training window is longer than the previous baseline's window with no full-collapse, OR the training window matches and end-of-run illegal_mass_sum / max_prob are at least as good as baseline.

NEUTRAL when:
- Same window, similar end-of-run health metrics. Baseline holds.

REGRESSED when:
- Early-bail on legal_mass_collapse, OR
- End-of-run full collapse signals (illegal_mass_sum ≥ 0.99 AND max ≥ 0.99 AND top1_legal_ever_positive == FALSE), OR
- Window shrank below baseline.

Bias proposals toward aggressive extensions of `training_time_seconds` over the previous accepted run when the previous run was healthy at its full window — at least +300s, but feel free to multiply (e.g. 600s → 1800s → 3600s → 5400s). When extending, also consider the parameters whose effect is dominated by late-training dynamics — entropy_bonus, lr_warmup_steps, weight_decay, and replay_ratio_target — since those govern whether the policy stays diverse deep into a long run. Early-abort thresholds in parameters.json are intentionally not overly aggressive; trust them.

Do your best!
