Raise entropy_bonus from 0.001 to 0.02 (20x). The trajectory shows the policy was healthily improving through t=290s (illegal mass falling from 0.996 to 0.798, a legal move rising above uniform), then after an arena/promotion the self-play workers started seeding the replay buffer with positions drawn from an already-peaky champion, and gradient descent drove the logits one-hot onto a single illegal move. The core problem is insufficient counter-pressure against peaky distributions: with entropy_bonus=0.001 the entropy term contributes negligible gradient compared to the cross-entropy term reinforcing whatever move the (now collapsed) champion picked. Bumping to 0.02 adds a meaningful -H(p) penalty that resists one-hot collapse without preventing the network from eventually learning sharp distributions on well-understood positions — this is the standard AlphaZero-style knob for exactly this failure mode, and 0.02 is in the conventional range (lc0 uses ~0.008–0.03). Changing only this one parameter isolates the effect so the next iteration can judge whether more (raise further) or less (the fix was elsewhere) is needed.
